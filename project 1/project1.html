A Classic Tamale

Masa, a nixtamalized maize dough used to make tamales, is heated in a corn husk or on banana leaves. Depending on regional and individual preferences, the filling can be produced from a variety of meats, cheeses, vegetables, chiles, or other preparations. Before eating, the wrapper can either be thrown away or used as a dish.

Chilaquiles

A typical Mexican breakfast dish called "chilaquiles" consists of crunchy tortilla chips topped with salsa, shredded cheese, and Mexican crema. The two most common salsas are salsa Roja (red) and salsa Verde (green), which are used to make Chilaquiles Rojos and Chilaquiles Verdes, respectively. The salsas used to cover the tortillas might vary. Frequently, the dish is served with extra protein in the form of fried eggs or chopped up chicken.

Tampiquena

The Tampico Club in Mexico City, founded in 1939, is where the Mexican steak preparation method known as "tampiquena" first emerged. The dish consists of a seasoned and grilled skirt steak that is accompanied by a number of sides and toppings, including cheese, roasted green chile, beans, rice, and corn tortillas. In contrast to carne asada, tampiquena is served as a complete steak with a focus on softness.
